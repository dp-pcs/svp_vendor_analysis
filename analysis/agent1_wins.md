## Where I Stand Firm (And Why)

### 1. M&A Department — RSM UK, Houlihan Lokey, Vector Capital, Westbrook Advisers, SS&C Intralinks

**My position:** M&A
**Agent 2's position:** Finance

**Why I'm right:** The Config taxonomy explicitly includes M&A as a separate department. If it were meant to roll into Finance, it wouldn't be a standalone category. These vendors are not accounting firms or tax advisors — they are:
- **RSM UK Corporate Finance** — restructuring, transactions, deal advisory
- **Houlihan Lokey** — investment bank, M&A advisory, valuations
- **Vector Capital** — private equity/investment management
- **Westbrook Advisers** — M&A advisory
- **SS&C Intralinks** — virtual data room software for M&A transactions

All five are transactional in nature, not operational finance. Putting them in Finance conflates two distinct spend categories and obscures the fact that this company has significant M&A advisory costs — which is strategically important context for an SVP of Operations doing post-acquisition due diligence.

**Practical impact:** If all M&A vendors get filed under Finance, the Finance department appears ~$240K larger than it is, and the M&A advisory cost line disappears entirely — exactly the line an acquirer most needs to see clearly.

### 2. Telefonica Global Services — G&A vs Engineering/SaaS

**My position:** G&A
**Agent 2's position:** Engineering

**Why I'm right (or at least defensible):** Telefonica Global Services is a telecom carrier providing enterprise connectivity, mobile, and business communications. This isn't dev tooling or cloud infrastructure — it's a phone/connectivity bill at the enterprise level. Most companies classify telecom spend under G&A (general operations overhead), not Engineering.

That said — if the spend is specifically for network infrastructure, IoT, or cloud services from Telefonica Tech, Engineering or SaaS would be valid. Without knowing the invoice breakdown, G&A is the safer default for a telecom carrier.

### 3. Aetna Life and Casualty — G&A vs Support

**My position:** G&A
**Agent 2's position:** Support

**Why I'm right:** Health insurance premiums are an employee benefits cost — they're procured by HR/G&A, administered by HR/G&A, and sit in G&A on the P&L of every company I've seen. "Support" in the Config taxonomy refers to customer support tooling (helpdesk, ticketing, live chat) — not HR benefits. Aetna doesn't support the company's customers; it insures the company's employees.

The vendor sells health insurance. The buyer is HR/G&A. The budget owner is G&A. Every comparable taxonomy puts benefits spend in G&A or HR. Since HR isn't a Config category, G&A is correct.

**This is not a coin flip.** Agent 2 is wrong here.

Agent 2 is trigger-happy on Consolidate.

### Pattern 3: Agent 2 ignores M&A as a category
Agent 2 used M&A zero times. A category was explicitly provided in the Config tab for M&A spend. A post-acquisition due diligence exercise where M&A advisory costs are invisible is a meaningful gap.
