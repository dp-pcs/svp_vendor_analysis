# Top 3 Strategic Cost-Saving Opportunities

## Executive Summary

Analysis of 386 vendors across $7.9M in annual spend identified three high-impact opportunities totaling **$623K–$1.0M** in potential annual savings, executable within 90 days of close.

---

## Opportunity 1: Salesforce Contract Renegotiation

**Summary Title:** CRM Platform Right-Sizing and Contract Renegotiation
**Department:** Sales
**Annual Spend:** $3,117,226

Salesforce is the single largest vendor at $3.1M — 40% of total vendor spend. This level of concentration is both a risk and an opportunity. Post-acquisition is the strongest negotiating position available: the acquirer can offer expanded seat volume across the broader group, benchmark against HubSpot and Microsoft Dynamics alternatives, and leverage Salesforce's well-known end-of-quarter close pressure. License audits at acquired companies routinely surface 20–30% unused or underutilized seats.

| Scenario | Rate | Annual Savings |
|---|---|---|
| Conservative (license audit + renegotiation) | 10% | $311,722 |
| **Base case** | **15%** | **$467,584** |
| Aggressive (volume expansion + competitive leverage) | 20% | $623,445 |

**Implementation:**
- Days 1–30: Pull contract terms, seat counts, active vs. inactive users, renewal date
- Days 30–45: Competitive benchmark via Vendr; get HubSpot/Dynamics quotes for leverage
- Days 45–90: Renegotiate anchored to group volume expansion and renewal timing

**Risk:** Salesforce may delay. Mitigation: initiate 90 days before renewal; have credible alternative identified.

**Estimated Annual Savings: $312K–$623K**

**Combined Spend:** $861,841 (11+ workspace vendors)

| Zagrebtower D.O.O. | $183,754 | Croatia |

| Work Easy Space Solutions | $14,913 | India |

Post-acquisition, workspace needs shift as headcount changes, hybrid policies reduce per-seat requirements, and the acquirer typically imposes a global real estate strategy. Two providers in Croatia, two in India, and multiple UK/Australia arrangements signal fragmented procurement with no central oversight. Consolidation to preferred providers per region + a utilization audit yields 17–29% savings.

| Action | Affected Spend | Savings | Annual Savings |
|---|---|---|---|
| Croatia: 2 providers → 1 | $327,847 | 25% | $81,962 |
| India: 2 providers → 1 | $162,261 | 20% | $32,452 |
| Utilization review (all regions) | $861,841 | 10% | $86,184 |
| **Total** | | | **$200,598** |

**Implementation:**
- Days 1–30: Audit headcount vs. capacity at each location; identify <60% utilization
- Days 30–60: Exit lowest-utilization locations; begin regional consolidation
- Days 60–90: Renegotiate remaining leases; pursue multi-location discounts

**Risk:** Lease termination penalties. Mitigation: legal review before action; prioritize month-to-month arrangements.

**Estimated Annual Savings: $150K–$250K**

## Opportunity 3: Professional Services & Advisory Consolidation

**Summary Title:** Accounting, Legal, and Advisory Firm Rationalization
**Departments:** Finance, Legal, M&A, Professional Services
**Combined Spend:** ~$641K across 25+ firms

| Category | Key Vendors | Spend |
|---|---|---|
| Primary Audit | BDO LLP | $343,081 |
| M&A Advisory | RSM UK Corporate Finance | $117,078 |
| Secondary Audit | Grant Thornton | $46,539 |
| Croatian Legal | Zuric I Partneri | $24,135 |
| Primary Legal | Bisley Law Ltd | $67,414 |
| Big 4 | PwC LLP | $4,879 |
| Regional Accounting | Crowe Horwath, Collards, others | $20,000+ |
| Other Legal (13+ firms) | Various | $18,342 |

An acquired company maintaining relationships with 25+ professional services, legal, and advisory firms is paying for fragmentation. Multiple audit firms, 13+ legal firms, and several overlapping advisory relationships create fee duplication and surrender volume leverage. BDO at $343K is already the primary audit relationship — consolidating secondary firms under BDO, rationalizing legal to 3–4 primary firms by geography, and eliminating post-acquisition M&A advisory costs yields meaningful savings.

| Action | Affected Spend | Savings | Annual Savings |
|---|---|---|---|
| Consolidate secondary audit firms | $71,389 | 40% | $28,556 |
| Legal firm rationalization (13 → 4) | $109,891 | 25% | $27,473 |
| Volume discount on primary firms | $343,081 | 10% | $34,308 |
| Regional consolidation | $22,226 | 30% | $6,668 |
| **Total** | | | **$97,005** |

**Implementation:**
- Days 1–30: Map which firm handles which work; identify overlapping scope
- Days 30–60: Consolidate secondary audit/accounting to BDO; reduce legal roster to primary firms by region
- Days 60–90: Renegotiate primary relationships with new combined volume

**Risk:** Relationship dependencies on specific partners; some firms may handle specialized compliance. Mitigation: maintain specialist relationships where genuinely required; consolidate generalist work first.

**Estimated Annual Savings: $97K–$150K**
